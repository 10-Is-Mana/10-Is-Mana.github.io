Hello!

Hi, this is Mana. I am a 2nd-year master's student in the Faculty of Systems Design at Tokyo Metropolitan University, advised by Mamoru Komachi. Before coming to TMU, I received a Bachelor of Arts (Linguistics major) at International Christian University. To put it in a nutshell, I study how language models behave. My interests are in the language, how a human acquires it, or a machine, say, a neural network learns something out of it. I am also engaging in the fairness of Artificial Intelligence by examining the potential adversarial consequences of AI usage against disadvantaged groups.

SIGTYP Newsletter, April 2021

I am glad to be invited to contribute to the SIGTYP April 2021 Newsletter. SIGTYP is one of the special interest groups in the ACL community, which focuses on Language Typology. The work we introduced is about Drenjongke, the language spoken in Sikkim, India. Before this work, our group was working mainly on the Drenjongke sound. The recordings collected during the past fieldwork can be downloaded upon request from the website. Some audio files, namely nursery rhymes, are uploaded on YouTube along with their lyrics and English translation. We are now working on developing a post-correction model of the OCR for Drenjongke. Stay tuned!

Sustainable Web Development

Have you noticed "Website Carbon Badge" on my website's home? As one of the researchers who are working in the AI related field, I am aware that the increasing use of computational resources for training models is a huge problem. It is often the case for any things that the problems of sustainability or of ethics is less concerned than performances, so as the case of the web (which is another point that is pretty interesting and important, but I am not going deeper this time.). Even though the things that I can do are limited, here as a one of such way, I put the Badge to raise people's awareness. Another thing I adopted is using Ecosia for my default browser. I will let you know as I found other ways to "engage" in this issue.
